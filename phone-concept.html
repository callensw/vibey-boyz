01

The Problem

Every smartphone in existence is a surveillance device with a phone app attached. iOS and Android are platforms built to extract — your location, your behavior, your attention, your money. The hardware is designed to be opaque. The software is designed to be dependent. The ecosystem is designed to be inescapable.

Workarounds exist. GrapheneOS gets you close. But you're still running Google silicon with no hardware kill switches, no certainty about what's happening below the OS layer, and a constant battle against an ecosystem that wasn't built for you.

Core Insight

The app era is ending. In 5–10 years, a fluid LLM interface will replace the app grid entirely. Whoever ships the first truly private, locally-intelligent device owns the next platform. Apple and Google cannot build it — their entire business model requires the opposite.

02

The Vision

"A device that is genuinely yours. Hardware you can verify. Software you can audit. Intelligence that runs locally and never leaves the device. No ads, no telemetry, no permission theater."

SOVEREIGN is a privacy-first smartphone built around a local LLM as the primary interface layer. There is no app grid. You interact with your device through natural language and fluid context-aware responses. The device knows you — and only the device knows you.

03

Core Differentiators

🔴

Hardware Kill Switches

Physical switches for microphone, camera, radios (cellular/WiFi/BT), and GPS. Not software toggles. Real circuit breaks.

🧠

Local LLM as OS

A compact, quantized model (7B–13B) runs on-device. It is the primary interface. No cloud dependency, no data leaving the device.

🔓

Fully Open Stack

Open hardware schematics, open firmware, open OS. Every layer auditable. No black boxes from baseband to UI.

📵

No Telemetry. Ever.

Zero analytics, zero crash reporting phoning home, zero behavioral tracking. The device has no commercial interest in your behavior.

⚡

Fluid LLM UI

The home screen is a conversation. Apps become capabilities. The model routes your intent to the right function without you navigating.

🛡️

Call Intelligence

Local model identifies unknown callers, auto-handles spam with legal warnings, transcribes and summarizes voicemails. No carrier needed.

The Baseband Problem

The hardest unsolved problem in truly private phones. The cellular radio requires closed-source firmware on virtually all current chipsets. Short-term mitigation: isolate baseband on separate silicon with no memory access to main OS. Long-term: partner with or fund open baseband development (Fairphone, Purism paths have shown it's possible, just slow).

05

The LLM Interface — How It Works

The home screen is a persistent, context-aware conversation with a local model that knows your device deeply. It replaces the concept of "opening apps."

"Call my sister"

Model accesses local contacts, places call. No dialer app needed.

"Unknown caller — deal with it"

Model answers, identifies spam or legitimate, handles appropriately. Logs and summarizes.

"What do I have tomorrow?"

Reads local calendar. No Google Calendar sync. No cloud.

"Navigate to the airport"

Routes via on-device maps + GPS. No Google Maps reporting your movement.

"Draft a reply to that email"

Model reads local email, drafts response. You review, send.

"What did jrmount text me last week?"

Searches local message history. Everything stays on device.

Go-To-Market

Phase 1 · Now → 12 months

Software-First Proof of Concept

Build the LLM UI layer on existing GrapheneOS + Pixel hardware. Demonstrate the interface concept without custom hardware. Validate demand.

Phase 2 · Year 1–2

Hardware Partner + Funding

Identify ODM manufacturing partner (likely Taiwan/Shenzhen). Raise seed round targeting privacy-focused investors, OSS foundations. Design kill switch hardware integration.

Phase 3 · Year 2–3

Developer Edition

Ship 1,000–5,000 units to technical early adopters. Rough edges acceptable. Build community, open-source the stack, get external security audits.

Phase 4 · Year 3–5

Consumer Launch

Polished device targeting mainstream privacy-conscious consumers. App compatibility layer mature. LLM interface refined from real-world usage data (collected locally, shared optionally).

Target Market

Initially: security researchers, journalists, privacy advocates, degoogle community (GrapheneOS has 50K+ active installs as a floor). Expansion: anyone who's tired of being the product. The market is everyone — the wedge is the early adopter.
